Highlights from Dr. Martin Luther King Jr's Life

  • Martin luther King Jr (MLK) was born on January 15, 1929 in Atlanta, Georgia.
  • Around 1935, Martin Luther King Jr. learned about the history of slavery and racism in America.
  • MLK memorized and sang hymns, and stated verses from the Bible by the time he was 5 years old.
  • in 1939, MLK sang as a memeber of his church choir in a slave costume, for the all-white audience.
  • MLK survived 2 attempts at suicide while believing he was the cause of his grandmothers injuries/death.
  • King became the youngest assistant manager of a newspaper delivery station at the age of 13 in 1942.
  • MLK narrowly escaped death on September 20th, 1958
  • MLK senteced to 4 months of hard labor on October 25th due to a probation plea deal his lawyer made previously.
  • 1963 MLK was arrested for protesting the treatment of blacks in Birmingham.
  • MLK made his famous "I have a Dream" speech on August 28th, 1963.
  • Martin Luther King Jr was assassinated in 1968 and died on Aprin 4th, 1968.
